The Hardest Year

A Love Story in Letters from Vietnam: This hybrid memoir is written mainly from the point of view of a young military wife during the Vietnam Era. Over 68,000 words are based on three hundred letters between the wife and her soldier husband, including their poetry, stories, and photographs. It has been professionally edited. Synopsis: In 1968, a nineteen-year-old college student, Carole, hastily married her college sweetheart one day before shipping out to Vietnam. At the same time the war raged around her husband, Bill, a cultural war brewed across Carole’s college campus, UW-Madison. But, through regular correspondence, the couple soon discovered that sustaining love in the midst of separation was their ultimate battle. Laura Colbert Naylor, Iraq war veteran and author of Sirens: How to Pee Standing Up, had this to say about The Hardest Year, “Carole and Bill’s hardest year is one for the history books. Carole’s saved correspondence between her and her husband, Bill, exemplifies the raw emotions, new and fleeting love, and moral injury that soldiers endure. Bill’s heroic time at war and Carole’s tumultuous encounters on the UW-Madison campus during the riots and protests parallel each other as they maneuver through the infancy of their marriage. Through their letters, you get a sense of Bill’s comrades jeering him along and his values and ethos winning that war, but in the process, losing a sense of being and his love of life. This book is a true transportation into the Vietnam era both from a civilian and a military lens.” Biographies Carole Wagener, a charter member of Coastal Dunes (a branch of the California Writers Club), has written for seven years and has had short stories and poetry published in four anthologies. The Hardest Year is her first book. She is a retired physical therapist who frequently worked with veterans. She and Bill have been married fifty-three years and have five children.
